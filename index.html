Brief History of the Venue

Canadian National (CN) Railway James Street Station (as LIUNA Station was formerly called) was built to address overcrowding at Grand Trunk Railway Stuart St. Station from Hamilton’s population growth in the early 20th Century. The first passenger train left James Street Station officially opened in May 1931, though the first passenger train left the station over a year earlier. Similar to other commercial and public buildings of the period, the station was built in the neoclassical style.

James Street Station was used steadily from the early 1930s until the early 1960s when passenger traffic fell. This downturn was a result of Highway 401’s expansion. To remain competitive by reducing passenger travel times, CN removed Hamilton as a stop from its mainline routes. These developments, coupled with the end of railroad mail service, saw a decline in the station’s use. With the transfer of passenger services from CN to GO and VIA, subsequent government cuts, and the VIA’s move to the Aldershot station in 1992, James Street Station closed in 1993.

James Street Station remained empty until 1996 when Hollywood producers offered CN a million dollars to renovate the station to shoot the movie The Long Kiss Goodnight (1996). The Labourer’s International Union of North America (LIUNA) noticed this new attention and purchased the station from CN, renaming it LIUNA Station. An additional $3 million was slotted for repairs and renovation. LIUNA Station was then reopened as the conference and banquet facility it is today. LIUNA Station continues to be used as a shooting location by the television and film industry. For example, it can be seen in X-Men (2000), The Good Witch (2015), Designated Survivor (2016), Titans (2018), and The Umbrella Academy (2019).
